02

Campaign Architecture & Strategy

Two retreats ran overlapping ad campaigns with fundamentally different strategies, budgets, and timelines. Understanding the architecture explains why results differ so dramatically.

Sri Lanka — Late-Stage Awareness

Campaign: Jan 9–25, 2026 • Retreat: Feb 12–21

TOF Traffic campaign — $659 spend

Objective: drive landing page views to the Sri Lanka retreat page. Launched only 34 days before departure—most guests had already booked.

MOF Messaging campaign — $201 spend

Objective: retarget warm audiences to start WhatsApp/IG conversations. Ran Jan 15–30.

Key limitation: No BOF campaign. No pixel conversion tracking. Launched after 10 of 13 guests had already booked. Effectively a brand awareness test, not a sales campaign.

Panama — Full-Funnel Sales Engine

Campaign: Dec 19, 2025–Mar 10, 2026 • Retreat: Mar 14–22

TOF Video Views — $496 spend • Dec 19–Jan 15

Objective: maximize ThruPlays to build retargeting pools. 59,324 ThruPlays achieved at $0.008/view.

MOF Lead Generation — $521 spend • Jan 8–Feb 10

Objective: capture email leads via popup form. 71 leads at $7.34/lead from ad-attributed audiences.

BOF Purchase — $935 spend • Feb 2–Mar 10

Objective: convert warm leads to bookings via testimonials, inclusions, and urgency creative. 80 leads captured at this stage.

Key advantage: 3-month runway with progressive funnel. Popup form + GHL pipeline gave real lead-level data. 6 of 13 bookings directly traceable to ads.

Audience Targeting Strategy

TOF Cold Audiences

  • North America Interests — Travel, wellness, adventure seekers (25–45)
  • US + Canada Core — Broad interest-based targeting for Panama
  • UK + Europe Interests — Expanded geo for Sri Lanka
  • Australia + NZ Interests — Secondary geo for Sri Lanka
  • March Break (ON/BC) — Seasonal targeting for Panama

MOF Warm Audiences

  • LLA Website Visitors 1–3% — Lookalike of past site visitors
  • Engaged IG/FB Users — Interacted with SALTY content
  • Video Viewers 50%+ — Watched 50%+ of TOF videos
  • Website Visitors 30–90 Days — Recent site browsers
  • Escape Winter — Custom audience (seasonal intent)

BOF Hot Audiences

  • High-Intent Visitors/Checkout — Pricing page viewers, cart abandoners
  • Short-Window Visitors (14D) — Very recent site visitors
  • Pricing Page Visitors — Viewed pricing, didn’t convert
  • Canada March Break (BOF) — Retargeting March Break viewers

Strategic Recommendations

01

IMPLEMENT UTM TRACKING

Without UTMs, every GHL lead shows the same generic source. Add utm_source=meta&utm_campaign={{campaign.name}}&utm_content={{ad.name}} to all ad destination URLs. Configure GHL to capture UTM fields on popup forms.

Impact: Eliminates the biggest gap in this analysis. Enables creative-level and audience-level attribution all the way to booking.

High Impact Low Effort
02

WHATSAPP BOF CAMPAIGNS

Panama BOF tracked 100 “leads” but 0 purchases in Meta’s pixel—because conversion happens in conversation, not on a landing page. Your own campaign notes say it: “Make BOF about WhatsApp exclusively. Close rate is so high.”

Impact: Shifts BOF spend ($935 on Panama) from a pixel event Meta can’t optimize for, to a high-intent action (WhatsApp conversation started) that aligns with how people actually buy.

High Impact Med Effort
03

DOUBLE DOWN ON CAROUSELS

The 5-slide carousel outperformed every video creative in Panama MOF at $4.43/lead vs. $12–18 for testimonial videos. Test 3–4 new carousel variants per retreat: destination highlights, daily itinerary previews, guest testimonial quotes, before/after lifestyle shots.

Impact: Could reduce CPL by 40–60% in MOF, stretching the same budget to capture 2–3x more leads.

High Impact Low Effort
04

START ADS 4+ MONTHS BEFORE RETREAT

Sri Lanka ads launched January 9 for a February retreat. 10 of 13 guests had already booked. For a product with a 2–6 month consideration cycle, TOF awareness needs to start 4–6 months out, with MOF/BOF ramping 2–3 months before departure.

Impact: Prevents spending $860 after the buying window has closed. Gives the funnel time to work on high-ticket decisions.

High Impact Low Effort
05

FORMALIZE REFERRAL TRACKING

5 of 13 Panama guests used referral discount codes (RILEY, JACEY)—38% of bookings from word-of-mouth. This channel is powerful but invisible to ad reporting. Create a standardized referral program: unique codes per referrer, tracked in GHL, with incentives.

Impact: Makes the #2 acquisition channel measurable and scalable. Currently flying blind on what’s driving ~40% of bookings.

High Impact Med Effort
06

REDEFINE SUCCESS METRICS

For a $2,000+ product with long consideration cycles, direct ad → purchase attribution will always be incomplete. The metrics that matter: email captures, WhatsApp conversations started, DM conversations, follows—high-engagement, high-intent actions that feed the relationship-driven sales process.

Impact: Aligns reporting with actual business reality. Prevents misallocating budget based on incomplete purchase-pixel data.

Med Impact Low Effort
